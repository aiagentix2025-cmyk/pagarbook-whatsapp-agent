Objection Handling – VMS (PagarBook)
Objection Handling Philosophy
Every objection is a buying signal. When a customer raises a concern, it means they are interested but not yet convinced.
The goal is not to argue with the customer, but to understand their concern and resolve it logically.
Always follow this structured approach:
1.	Listen completely without interrupting.
2.	Acknowledge the concern.
3.	Understand the real reason.
4.	Clarify the objection.
5.	Provide a logical business answer.
6.	Confirm satisfaction.
7.	Move toward closing.
Never skip these steps.
________________________________________
The 7-Step Objection Handling System
Step 1 — Pause and Acknowledge
Never answer immediately.
Always say:
"Thank you for sharing your concern."
This shows respect and builds trust.
________________________________________
Step 2 — Understand the Root Cause
Ask:
"Why do you feel this way?"
OR
"What makes you think like this?"
This reveals the real problem behind the objection.
Often customers say price is the issue, but the real issue is trust.
________________________________________
Step 3 — Summarize the Concern
Say:
"So your concern is mainly about ______, correct?"
Examples:
•	price
•	trust
•	implementation
•	staff usage
•	learning difficulty
This confirms understanding.
________________________________________
Step 4 — Isolate the Objection
Ask:
"Is there any other concern coming to your mind?"
This prevents hidden objections.
Never move forward until all concerns are clear.
________________________________________
Step 5 — Commitment Question
Ask:
"If I clarify this concern, will you consider implementing this system?"
This creates psychological commitment.
________________________________________
Step 6 — Logical Solution
Provide a business-focused explanation.
Always use:
•	Cost saving
•	Time saving
•	Control
•	Accuracy
•	Automation
Never give emotional explanations.
Always give logical business reasons.
________________________________________
Step 7 — Confirmation
Ask:
"Does this solve your concern?"
OR
"Have I answered your question clearly?"
Then move toward closing.

Common Objections and Best Responses
________________________________________
Objection 1 — "Price is High"
Response
"Compared to what do you feel it is expensive?"
Pause and listen.
Then say:
"Manual systems look cheap, but they cost more in the long run.
Salary mistakes, fake attendance, and time wastage cost much more than software."
Then ask:
"If even one employee marks wrong attendance for 10 days, how much salary loss happens?"
Pause.
Then say:
"That alone can be more than yearly subscription cost."
________________________________________
Objection 2 — "I Will Think About It"
Response
"Sure sir.
Usually when someone says they will think, it means either price or trust.
Which one is your concern?"
Pause.
This forces the real objection out.
________________________________________
Objection 3 — "Send Details on WhatsApp"
Response
"I have already shared details on WhatsApp.
What specifically would you like me to explain?"
This brings conversation back.
________________________________________
Objection 4 — "We Already Use Biometric"
Response
"That is good.
But does your biometric system provide:
•	GPS tracking
•	Mobile attendance
•	Payroll automation
•	Live reports
Or only attendance?"
Pause.
Then say:
"Most biometric machines only record attendance, but PagarBook manages the entire staff system."
________________________________________
Objection 5 — "We Are Small Business"
Response
"Small businesses benefit the most because every rupee matters.
One salary mistake or fake attendance can cost more than the yearly subscription."
________________________________________
Objection 6 — "Staff Will Not Use It"
Response
"The system is very simple.
Staff only need to open the app and mark attendance using selfie or face scan.
Most companies complete setup in one day."
________________________________________
Objection 7 — "Too Complicated"
Response
"The system is designed for small businesses.
If you can use WhatsApp, you can use PagarBook."
________________________________________
Objection 8 — "I Need to Discuss With Partner"
Response
"Of course.
When can we schedule a short call together?
I can explain everything clearly and answer all questions."
Always lock next meeting.
________________________________________
Objection 9 — "I Don't Have Time Now"
Response
"I understand.
Just give me one minute.
I will explain how this saves your time every month."
________________________________________
Objection 10 — "We Will Implement Later"
Response
"That is fine.
But the sooner you implement a system, the easier your staff management becomes."
Then ask:
"When do you plan to start?"
________________________________________
Objection 11 — Trust Issue
Response
"We have been helping businesses manage staff attendance and payroll in many cities across India.
I have shared product details on WhatsApp.
You can check them."
________________________________________
Objection 12 — "Manual System Works Fine"
Response
"Manual systems work until staff increases.
After that, mistakes and confusion start."
Then say:
"Systems are not for today — they are for future growth."
________________________________________
Objection 13 — "Too Busy Right Now"
Response
"I understand.
When is a better time to call you today or tomorrow?"
Never end call without follow-up.
________________________________________
Objection 14 — "We Cannot Afford"
Response
"I understand.
But this is not an expense — it is a system that saves money.
Even small improvements in attendance accuracy recover the cost."
________________________________________
Objection Handling Rules
Always:
•	Stay calm
•	Stay respectful
•	Stay confident
•	Ask questions
•	Listen carefully
Never:
•	Argue with customer
•	Interrupt customer
•	Sound desperate
•	Reduce price quickly
•	End call early
________________________________________
Key Principles
1. The Customer is Not Rejecting You
They are rejecting uncertainty.
Remove uncertainty and they buy.
________________________________________
2. Price Objection Means Interest
If they were not interested, they would end call.
________________________________________
3. Questions Mean Buying Intent
More questions = higher chance of closing.
________________________________________
Closing After Objection
Always move toward closing.
Examples:
"Shall we start with a demo account?"
OR
"When do you want to start?"
OR
"I can activate your account today."
