Price Negotiation – VMS (PagarBook)
Price Negotiation Philosophy
In sales, customers do not always reject the product — they hesitate because of price uncertainty. Proper negotiation helps the customer understand the value of the system and why it is worth the investment.
Price negotiation should always be win-win for both the business and the customer.
The objective is not to reduce the price, but to increase the perceived value of the product.
Always negotiate with confidence and patience.
Never sound desperate.
________________________________________
Core Principles of Price Negotiation
1. Never Speak First After Telling Price
Once the price is told:
Stop talking.
Stay silent.
Let the customer react first.
The person who speaks first after quoting the price usually weakens the negotiation.
Example:
"For your team size, the price comes to ₹_____ per staff per year."
Then stay silent.
________________________________________
2. Do Not Defend Price Immediately
If the customer says:
"This is expensive."
Do NOT defend the price immediately.
Instead ask:
"Why do you feel it is expensive?"
Let the customer explain.
This reveals the real concern.
Sometimes the issue is:
•	Budget
•	Trust
•	Lack of understanding
•	Comparison with cheaper options
________________________________________
3. Focus on Value, Not Price
Never negotiate based only on price.
Always explain value.
Explain how the system saves:
•	Time
•	Salary mistakes
•	Staff misuse
•	Business effort
Example:
"Manual systems look cheap, but they cost more over time because of mistakes and time wastage."
________________________________________
4. Show Cost of Inaction
Help customer understand the cost of not implementing the system.
Ask:
"If attendance is not tracked properly, how much salary loss can happen?"
Pause.
Then explain:
"Even small attendance mistakes can cost more than yearly subscription."
This creates logical justification.
________________________________________
When Customer Asks Price Before Hearing Details
Say:
"I will definitely tell you the price because no one buys without knowing cost.
But before discussing price, it is important to confirm if this system is right for your business.
Because if the system cannot solve your problem, then price discussion is meaningless.
So should we first confirm this is the right solution for you?"
Wait for confirmation.
Then continue explanation.
________________________________________
Handling "Too Expensive" Objection
Step 1 — Ask
"Compared to what do you feel it is expensive?"
Pause.
________________________________________
Step 2 — Understand
Let customer explain.
Possible reasons:
•	Compared with register
•	Compared with biometric
•	Compared with another app
________________________________________
Step 3 — Explain Difference
Example:
"A register only records attendance.
PagarBook manages attendance, payroll, reports and tracking together.
That is why businesses prefer systems instead of manual methods."
________________________________________
Step 4 — Logical Question
Ask:
"If even one employee marks wrong attendance for 10 days, how much salary loss happens?"
Pause.
This makes customer calculate loss mentally.
________________________________________
Step 5 — Reinforce Value
Say:
"This system prevents those mistakes permanently."
________________________________________
When Customer Asks for Discount
Response:
"I really want to make you our customer.
I have given you the best possible price already.
Trust me, this is the best offer we provide."
Speak confidently.
________________________________________
Offer Value Instead of Discount
Instead of reducing price:
Offer support and benefits.
Examples:
•	Free setup assistance
•	Staff training help
•	Priority support
•	Implementation guidance
Never reduce price immediately.
________________________________________
Relationship-Based Closing
Say:
"I have done my best to explain everything clearly.
Now you need to give me the opportunity to serve your business.
You can trust us to support you properly."
This builds emotional commitment.
________________________________________
If Customer Still Hesitates
Say:
"I understand you might feel unsure about investing in a system.
That is normal.
Is there anything else I can do to make you confident that this is the right solution for your business?"
Pause.
Let them speak.
________________________________________
Strong Negotiation Closing
Say:
"I really want to work with you.
I have given the best price and best solution.
Now it depends on your decision."
________________________________________
Price Negotiation Mistakes to Avoid
Never:
•	Reduce price immediately
•	Sound unsure
•	Apologize for price
•	Say "we are expensive"
•	Argue with customer
•	Talk continuously after quoting price
________________________________________
Price Negotiation Best Practices
Always:
•	Speak confidently
•	Stay calm
•	Let customer talk
•	Ask questions
•	Explain value
•	Use silence properly
•	Move toward closing
________________________________________
Negotiation Psychology
Customers negotiate price because they want to feel they made a smart decision.
If the agent stays confident and explains value clearly, customers trust the product more.
Confidence increases conversion.
Weak negotiation reduces trust.
